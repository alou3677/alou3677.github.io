Automotive Culture: Volvo

Mom's 2002 Volvo S40, my high school 2012 Volvo S60 T5, and now a 2015 Volvo S60 T5 AWD. I think it's safe to say that Volvo has been in my life for a very, very long time. For me, the biggest thing about Volvo that I love is that you just don't see that many of them on the roads (in America). And next up, Volvo really does make some decently fun engines (torque > hp) alongside some of the most comfortable seats in their range. All in all, Volvo is the unique carmaker that's trying to make some brave moves in the automotive world, and I like that.

Automotive Dream: McLaren

I don't know where or when the obsession began, but at some point in time, McLaren become one of the most important car brands in the world to me. One of the biggest reasons I quickly became very interested was because of Chris Harris' amazingly cinematic automotive reviews. And then it was just the raw fact that this British company was brave enough to challenge Ferrari with their first large-scale production road car. Cheers to you McLaren, wishing you all the best.

Sports: Swimming

I've been swimming for over 80% of my life, and of that, about half has been dedicated to swimming competitively on my year-round club team, the California Dolphin Swim Team based out of Fremont, CA. In high school, I may have taken swimming more seriously than my actual studies, but I learned a great deal from the pool and did end up with Sectionals cuts (bonus pts for you if you know what this means) for my best sprint freestyle events. Now that studies and work have taken over my life, my time in the pool has fallen significantly, but I still enjoy my time in the water.

Sports: Downhill Skiing

Snow, speed, and scenery. I'm not sure what else you could be asking for in the winter season, but that's all I need to enjoy the slopes. It's a family tradition of mine to go skiing in Tahoe every winter and you can often find us on the Heavenly slopes around Christmas time.
